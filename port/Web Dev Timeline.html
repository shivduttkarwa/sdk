A six-act ritual

BUILDING
THE WEB,
one strike
at a time.

Scroll to forge ↓ Est. read · 4 min
01

DISCOVER
& SCOPE

Every project begins in the dark — listening, asking, mapping the unknown.

We sit with the client until the problem stops being vague. Goals, audiences, constraints, the budget that nobody wants to say out loud — all of it gets pulled into the light.

By the end of this act we know what we're building, who it's for, and what would make it fail. No code yet. Just clarity, sharpened.

  • kick-off
  • research
  • scope doc
02

ARCHITECT
THE SHAPE

Information architecture, flows, the bones beneath the skin.

Sitemaps, user journeys, wireframes at low fidelity. We argue about navigation. We delete pages. We promise ourselves we will not build a mega-menu, then we discuss building a mega-menu.

The output is a blueprint the whole team can read — designers, devs, and the stakeholder who only opens PDFs.

  • IA
  • wireframes
  • flows
03

DESIGN
THE BLADE

Type, colour, motion, voice — the part you can finally show people.

Visual direction is set, components are forged in Figma, the design system gets a name. We pressure-test edge cases: empty states, error states, the long German word in the heading.

Nothing ships from this stage until a real human can navigate the prototype without us hovering behind them.

  • UI
  • system
  • prototype
04

BUILD
IN CODE

Components, routes, data, the slow click of pieces locking together.

Front-end and back-end meet at the API. We write tests we'll later thank ourselves for. Performance budgets get set early so they can be quietly broken later.

Every PR is a small ceremony — reviewed, merged, deployed to staging where it can be poked at safely.

  • frontend
  • backend
  • ci/cd
05

TEST
& TEMPER

Heat, water, repeat. Every blade earns its edge.

QA across browsers and devices. Accessibility audits with real assistive tech. Load testing the parts we secretly suspect won't survive a Tuesday.

Bugs are caught, sorted, ranked, killed. The ones we keep are the ones we chose to keep.

  • QA
  • a11y
  • perf
06

LAUNCH
& LIVE

The site goes live — and the real work begins.

DNS flips. Analytics fires. Someone in a different timezone finds the one bug we missed. We patch, we measure, we feed what we learn back into the next version.

A site is never really finished. It is only ever paused at a version you can be proud of.

  • monitor
